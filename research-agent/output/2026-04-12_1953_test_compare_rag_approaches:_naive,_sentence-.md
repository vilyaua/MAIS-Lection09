### Summary of Pros and Cons with Suitable Use Cases

**Naive RAG Approach**
- *Pros:* Easy to implement and understand; low overhead; integration with basic retrievers straightforward.
- *Cons:* May introduce irrelevant information; lacks contextual overlap leading to potential coherence loss; not ideal for complex or multi-hop queries.
- *Use Cases:* Lightweight applications, initial experimentation, short documents or queries with narrowly focused context.

**Sentence-Window RAG Approach**
- *Pros:* Preserves local sentence context better; overlapping windows capture transition between segments; improves retrieval quality and LLM input coherence; supports metadata enrichment.
- *Cons:* Increased retrieval and indexing size; additional complexity in chunk management and overlapping embedding; moderate increase in computation.
- *Use Cases:* Medium to large document collections, use cases requiring precise context, complex single-hop QA, summarization.

**Parent-Child RAG Approach**
- *Pros:* Best for maintaining document hierarchy and semantic relations; effective in multi-hop and reasoning tasks; reduces context noise by structurally filtering retrieval.
- *Cons:* Requires hierarchical metadata and sophisticated retrieval design; higher computational cost; more engineering effort.
- *Use Cases:* Multi-hop QA, long multi-section documents (legal, technical), knowledge bases with rich document structures, complex reasoning applications.
