1. Efficient Training via Adaptive Speculative Decoding (MIT TLT System)
A major breakthrough emerging in 2026 is a training efficiency method called "Taming the Long Tail" (TLT) developed by MIT and collaborators. Recognizing that reinforcement learning (RL) training of reasoning LLMs is bottlenecked by idle processor time during rollout operations (up to 85% time consumed), TLT opportunistically trains a smaller, faster draft model on idle processors. This draft model predicts outputs of the larger reasoning model—which then verifies these predictions. The draft model is adaptively and continuously updated alongside the larger model, ensuring alignment and maintaining accuracy. An adaptive rollout engine manages the speculative decoding configurations optimizing speed dynamically. This approach achieved 70% to 210% training speedup without loss of accuracy, reducing computational cost and energy usage. The small drafter model also doubles as an efficient deployment model.
